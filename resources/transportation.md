## 🎓 For **Matriculated U of M Student Interns**

### 🚍 **Blue Bus / U-M Transit Services**

* Free campus and Ann Arbor routing for all riders. ([Wikipedia][1])
* Routes cover Central Campus, North Campus, Michigan Medicine, and commuter lots.
* Real-time tracking and schedules available here:

  * [https://ltp.umich.edu/campus-transit/](https://ltp.umich.edu/campus-transit/) ([Logistics, Transportation & Parking][6])

**Best for:** Daily travel around campus without needing a car.

---

### 🚌 **TheRide / AAATA (City Buses)**

* As a U-M student with a **yellow MCard**, you can ride most fixed-route buses free under the **MRide Program** — just swipe your card when boarding. ([Logistics, Transportation & Parking][2])
* Includes connections beyond campus into Ann Arbor and nearby communities.

**Useful:**

* Fare & pass info (if needed): [https://www.theride.org/fares-passes/fares](https://www.theride.org/fares-passes/fares) ([theride.org][7])
* Full route schedules/maps: [https://www.theride.org/](https://www.theride.org/) ([theride.org][8])

---

### 🚗 **Parking Permits (If You Bring a Car)**

* Student permits (Orange, Yellow, Storage) available through LTP if eligible (usually for juniors, seniors, graduate students). ([Logistics, Transportation & Parking][9])
* Daily and virtual permits available. ([Logistics, Transportation & Parking][10])
* **Note:** Parking on campus is limited and expensive; public transit or bike/walk is recommended for most interns. ([parents.umich.edu][11])

---

### 🚴 **Bicycling & Pedestrian Options**

* Ann Arbor and U-M are bicycle friendly with racks and bike lanes. ([rackham.umich.edu][5])
* Walking and cycling are great options for short distances on and near campus.

---

### 📍 **Key Links for U-M Students**

## 🎒 For **Non-U of M Student Interns (from Other Institutions)**

### 🚌 **Blue Bus (U-M Transit Services)**

* Free to ride for **everyone**, including public visitors. ([Wikipedia][1])
* Useful within the U-M campus and to Michigan Medicine facilities.

---

### 🚌 **TheRide / AAATA (City & Regional Buses)**

* Public city buses around Ann Arbor and Ypsilanti.
* **Fares (2026 approx):**

  * One-way ride: $1.50
  * Day pass (unlimited rides for a day): ~$3.00
  * 30-day pass: ~$45.00 ([theride.org][7])

**Helpful links:**

* Fares & passes: [https://www.theride.org/fares-passes/fares](https://www.theride.org/fares-passes/fares) ([theride.org][7])
* Routes & schedules: [https://www.theride.org/](https://www.theride.org/) ([theride.org][8])

---

### 🚗 **Park & Ride Commuter Lots**

* Free park & ride lots around Ann Arbor give you a place to leave your car and catch a bus into town/campus. ([theride.org][3])
* Locations include Green Road, Plymouth Road, Miller Road, Pioneer High School, and more. ([theride.org][13])
* Bus fare applies unless your organization offers a pass.

---

### 🅿️ **Visitor Parking Options**

* Several pay-to-park locations on and near campus — hourly rates apply and payment through AIMS Mobile Pay. ([Logistics, Transportation & Parking][4])
* Useful if you need short-term visits during your internship.

---

### 🚴 **Biking & Walking**

* Ann Arbor has bike lanes and pedestrian routes; biking is a cost-effective way to get around. ([rackham.umich.edu][5])

## 🏁 Tips for Summer Interns

* **No car? No problem:** U-M transit is robust and frequently connects central campus, North Campus, medical centers, and commuter lots. ([Wikipedia][1])
* **U-M Students:** Always carry your **yellow MCard** — it gives you **free rides on TheRide** buses. ([Logistics, Transportation & Parking][2])
* **Non-U-M Students:** Consider day or monthly passes on TheRide for regular commuting. ([theride.org][7])
* **Parking:** Even with paid options, on-campus parking is limited and costly — transit or two-wheels usually beats driving. ([parents.umich.edu][11])
