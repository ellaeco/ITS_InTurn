# 🏠 University of Michigan Summer Housing Guide for Interns

This guide outlines the **housing and related resources** available to students participating in a **University of Michigan summer internship program**, broken down by:

* **U-M enrolled students**, and
* **Visiting students** (interns matriculated at other institutions)

---

## 1. For U-M Enrolled Students

If you are **currently enrolled at the University of Michigan**, you are eligible for official **Michigan Housing summer options**.

### On-Campus Spring/Summer Housing

**Michigan Housing – Spring/Summer Term Student Housing**
[https://housing.umich.edu/spring-summer-term-student-housing/](https://housing.umich.edu/spring-summer-term-student-housing/)

**What it offers:**

* Residence hall housing (typically East Quadrangle)
* Available late May through August
* Fully furnished rooms
* Meal plans available
* Utilities and internet included

**Key notes:**

* Requires active U-M enrollment
* Applications usually open in **mid-March**
* Contracts are term-based (not nightly)

This is the simplest option for U-M students who want a **turnkey, on-campus experience**.

---

### Graduate & Family Housing (Longer Stays)

**Michigan Housing – Graduate Housing**
[https://housing.umich.edu/graduate-rates/](https://housing.umich.edu/graduate-rates/)

Includes Northwood Community Apartments and other units.

**Good fit for:**

* Graduate students
* Research interns
* Students staying the full summer or longer

---

### Cooperative & Off-Campus Options

**Inter-Cooperative Council (ICC)**
[https://icc.coop/housing/](https://icc.coop/housing/)

Student-run cooperative houses with:

* Lower cost
* Shared responsibilities
* Strong community vibe

**Off-Campus Sublets**

* Facebook groups (e.g. “UMich Off Campus Housing”)
* Craigslist / Apartments.com / Zillow

These are common for students who want:

* More independence
* Lower cost
* Short-term sublets (May–August)

---

## 2. For Visiting / Non-U-M Interns

If you are **not matriculated at U-M** but are participating in a U-M internship or program, you typically **cannot use standard Michigan Housing**.

Your primary on-campus option is:

### Intern Housing via Conference & Event Services

**U-M Conference & Event Services – Intern Housing**
[https://conferences.umich.edu/summer/intern-housing/](https://conferences.umich.edu/summer/intern-housing/)

**What it offers:**

* Furnished single rooms
* Nightly or monthly rates
* Utilities + internet included
* Laundry access
* Community kitchens
* Optional parking

**Typical availability:**

* Mid-May through early August
* Often requires a **30-day minimum stay**

**Best for:**

* Visiting interns
* Visiting researchers
* Summer program participants

This is the **closest equivalent to dorm housing** for non-U-M students.

---

### Off-Campus Options for Visiting Students

Most visiting interns also explore:

* **Private apartment sublets**
* **Short-term rentals**
* **ICC co-ops (case-by-case)**
* Airbnb / furnished corporate rentals

These give more flexibility but require more coordination.

## Practical Recommendations

### If you are a **U-M student**

Start with:

1. Michigan Housing summer application
2. ICC co-ops if budget-sensitive
3. Off-campus sublets if you want independence

### If you are a **visiting intern**

Start with:

1. Conference & Event Services Intern Housing
2. Off-campus sublets in Ann Arbor
3. Corporate/furnished rentals for short stays

## Timeline Tips

| When           | What to Do                                 |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| February–March | Check housing eligibility                  |
| Mid-March      | Apply for Michigan Housing (U-M students)  |
| March–April    | Contact Intern Housing (visiting students) |
| April–May      | Secure off-campus backup                   |

---

## Program Admin Tip (for Coordinators)

If you’re running an internship program (like ITS, research cohorts, or visiting fellowships):

* Pre-negotiate a **block of Intern Housing rooms** via Conference Services.
* Include housing links directly in your **offer letters**.
* Maintain a shared Google Sheet of sublets from current students.

This dramatically reduces intern onboarding friction.

---

## One-Line Decision Rule

> **U-M student?** Use Michigan Housing.
> **Not a U-M student?** Use Intern Housing or off-campus rentals.
