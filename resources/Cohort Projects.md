# Cohort Projetcs

## Overview

### What are Cohort Projects?

Cohort Projects are collaborative, intern-led initiatives that solve real problems submitted by departments across the University of Michigan. Each summer, internship project sponsors propose challenges for interns to address through research, design, development, process improvement, analytics, or other innovative solutions.

Each project consists of a team of approximately 5–7 interns, with one intern serving as the Project Manager (PM). Throughout the internship, teams work alongside a Project Sponsor and a Project Management Coach to deliver a final solution that is presented during the ITS Internship Showcase.

Why Cohort Projects Matter

Cohort Projects allow interns to:

* Work on real university challenges

* Gain experience working on cross-functional teams

* Develop leadership and project management skills

* Network with ITS professionals

* Learn new technologies and methodologies

* Deliver meaningful work that benefits the university

Departments benefit by receiving fresh perspectives and innovative solutions while mentoring the next generation of IT professionals.

### How Cohort Projects Work

* Sponsors submit project ideas before the internship begins.
  
* Interns attend sponsor presentations during orientation.\
  
* Interns are organzied into project teams.
  
* Teams create and present a Project Charge.
  
* Sponsors approve project scope.
  
* Teams execute project work over approximately 11 weeks.
  
* Weekly meetings and status reports keep everyone aligned.
  
* Projects conclude with documentation, turnover, and the Intern Showcase.

## Program Structure

### Interns

Interns are responsible for completing project work while balancing their departmental assignments and professional development activities.

#### Expectations

* Spend approximately 4–6 hours per week on cohort projects
  
* Attend project meetings

* Complete assigned tasks

* Collaborate with teammates
  
* Participate in planning and brainstorming
  
* Prepare project deliverables
  
* Present final work during the Internship Showcase

### Project Manager

Each project team has one intern serving as Project Manager.

#### Responsibilities

* Organize project work

* Break work into manageable tasks

* Coordinate schedules

* Facilitate project meetings

* Track progress

* Identify risks and issues

* Communicate with sponsors

* Submit weekly status reports

* Coordinate project closeout

#### What PMs Do NOT Do

Project managers are not:

* People managers
  
* Technical decision makers
  
* Requirement creators
  
* Primary deliverable owners

Instead, they focus on organizing work and removing blockers so the team can succeed.

#### Why Become a PM?

Serving as a PM provides opportunities to:

* Develop leadership skills

* Gain project management experience

* Build communication skills

* Learn stakeholder management

* Strengthen your resume

* Network with ITS leadership

### Project Sponsors

Project Sponsors act as the project owners and provide the vision for the team.

#### Before the Internship

Sponsors should:

* Meet weekly with the Project Manager or project team

* Review project progress

* Approve deliverables

* Provide timely feedback

* Clarify project requirements

* Monitor risks and project scope

#### End of Project

Sponsors should:

* Review final deliverables

* Accept project turnover documentation

* Attend final demonstrations

* Confirm project completion

#### Common Sponsors Challenges

**Engagement Gaps**

Projects often struggle when sponsors do not meet regularly with their teams.

**Resources Delays**

Ensure software licenses, hardware, and required access are available before work begins.

**Scope Creep**

If project work exceeds approximately six hours per week per intern, sponsors should work with the Internship Planning Team to reduce scope.

**Information Bottlenecks**

Clearly define requirements early to avoid confusion later in the project.

**Delayed Intervention**

Address risks immediately rather than waiting until the end of the internship.

### PM Coaches

Every Project Manager is assigned a Project Management Coach.

#### Coach Responsibilities

PM Coaches provide guidance and mentorship throughout the project.

They:

* Meet regularly with Project Managers

* Help identify risks

* Offer project management advice

* Share best practices

* Provide coaching on leadership and communication

PM Coaches Do NOT:

* Manage the project

* Attend regular sponsor meetings

* Complete project work

* Make project decisions

## Communication Expectations

Effective communication is essential throughout every project.

### Weekly Meetings

Sponsors should meet with the Project Manager or project team weekly to:

* Review progress

* Clarify requirements

* Resolve questions

* Discuss upcoming work

### Weekly Status Reports

Status reports should include:

* Progress completed

* Upcoming work
  
* Risks

* Issues

* Requests for sponsor assistance

Reports are typically submitted:

* Every Friday

* To the Internship Planning Team

* Project Sponsor

* Project Team

### Escalting Issues

Projects should escalate concerns early when experiencing:

* Sponsor communication issues

* Resource delays

* Team conflicts

* Scope concerns
