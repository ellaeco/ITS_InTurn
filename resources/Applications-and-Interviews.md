# Application 
Once the time comes to open applications for the internship, the website for accepting applications and the application itself needs to be created. Qualtrics is used to create and distribute the application for the ITS Internship.
## The application should include questions like:
* The name of the applicant
* The position they are applying for
* Contact details
* Employment history
* Availability throughout the summer
* Legal eligibiliy
* Age of the applicant
* School attended by the applicant and year
* Eligibility to work in the United States/Michigan
* How did the applicant hear about the position
The application should also have a place for the applicant to submit their resume and cover letter. The applicant should be able to submit it as a PDF or some kind of other separate document.
After the application deadlines close, one or more people should be assigned to review the applications and decide which applicants are qualified enough for an interview. These should be people familiar with the internship and its expectations of the interns. The reviewer will be reading the cover letter, the resume, looking at the applicant’s answers to the application’s questions, and determining if they would be a good fit for the internship. After a substantial number of applicants have been selected, it is time to start preparing for the interview process.
# Interviews 
The first thing that must be set up is a way to schedule the interviews with not just the applicants but the people that will be interviewing them. Create a SignUp Genius for the applicants that has all of the available interview spots so they can find one that works for them. 
Once all of the applicants have selected a timeslot, make sure that that time works for whoever will be their supervisor should they get accepted into the program. The interview should be conducted with someone from the planning team and a supervisor from the specific department the applicant is looking to work with. Next, questions to ask the applicants need to be developed. 
## Possible Questions 
* Tell us about a time you handled a stressful situation at work or school.
* Tell us about a time you had to adapt to a significant change at school or work and how you handled that situation.
* How do you prioritize your workload when dealing with multiple assignments?
* What are some of your skills you think will help you in this role?
Make sure the interviewers are taking notes while speaking with the candidates. After all of the interviews are conducted, have a conversation with all of the supervisors that were present during the interviews and determine which of the candidates would be best suited for the internship. Once the best of the candidates are selected, arrange for someone to call them and let them know they have been accepted into the program. Then send all of the interns and email about the next steps to getting started with the internship program.
