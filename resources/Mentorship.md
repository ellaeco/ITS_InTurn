# Mentorship Program

> A guide to help interns make the most of their mentorship experience.

---

## Purpose of the Mentorship Program

The Mentorship Program provides interns with one-to-one support from an assigned mentor. It is designed to help interns adjust to their roles, build confidence, and develop both professional and technical skills. Mentors also serve as a direct point of contact for questions, feedback, and ongoing support.

---

## Mentor Matching Process

Interns are assigned a mentor from the Michigan Technology Community during their first full week in the program.

A mentorship directory is available where interns can view mentor profiles and select their top three mentor preferences based on their interests and goals.

The Mentorship Committee reviews these preferences and assigns a mentor to each intern.

**Mentorship Directory:** [(https://internship.its.umich.edu/mentor-directory)]

> **Tip:** Share your mentor's name with your supervisor once assignments are finalized in case coordination or communication is needed.

---

## What to Expect

Interns can expect:

- One-to-one mentorship meetings
- Discussions about goals, progress, and challenges
- Guidance on professional and technical development
- Ongoing support throughout the internship

Mentors are available for scheduled check-ins and additional support as needed.

---

## Meetings

Mentors and interns meet regularly in one-to-one sessions. These meetings provide an opportunity to:

- Review progress
- Set and refine goals
- Discuss challenges and successes
- Receive guidance and feedback

> Meeting frequency may vary based on mentor and intern availability.

---

## Roles & Responsibilities

### Intern Responsibilities

- Attend scheduled mentorship meetings
- Actively participate in discussions
- Set and work toward professional goals
- Communicate needs, questions, and challenges

### Mentor Responsibilities

- Provide guidance and constructive feedback
- Support professional development and goal setting
- Conduct regular check-ins
- Help interns navigate their internship experience

### Mentorship Subcommittee Responsibilities

- Manage the mentor directory and assignments
- Support the mentor matching process
- Address concerns and escalations

---

## Communication Guidelines

- Primary communication between interns and mentors should be direct (email, Teams, or another designated platform)
- Communicate early if you need support or need to reschedule a meeting
- Be respectful of each other's time and availability

---

## Escalation Path

| Concern | Contact |
|----------|----------|
| Mentor-related concerns | Anyone from the Mentorship Subcommittee |
| Work-related concerns | Your Supervisor |
| Urgent or sensitive concerns | Program Lead (Claire Tucker) |

---

## Goals & Development

Mentorship includes setting and tracking professional goals. Interns are encouraged to:

- Define short-term and long-term goals
- Discuss progress during mentorship meetings
- Reflect on learning outcomes throughout the internship
- Seek feedback and identify areas for growth

---

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Can I change my mentor?

Yes. Requests can be submitted to the Mentorship Subcommittee.

### What if my mentor is unavailable?

Reach out to the Mentorship Subcommittee for support or reassignment.

### How often should meetings happen?

Meeting frequency depends on mentor and intern schedules, but regular check-ins are encouraged.
