# Frequently Asked Questions

## Is there a dress code for on-site work?
Most of ITS follows “business casual” for in-person programming. Once you begin working, observe what others wear to gauge the appropriate attire. For events like a tour of a local business or our Intern Showcase at the end of the program, you should opt for business professional.

A key aspect of this internship program is introducing you to the professional world and providing opportunities to network with potential career mentors. Dressing appropriately for these occasions is important to make a positive impression.

## How is the mentorship structured?
Interns can access our mentor directory to choose potential mentors and rank their preferences after orientation day. Once pairs are established, interns can begin meeting with their mentors. While the frequency of these meetings is flexible and can be determined jointly by the intern and mentor, we recommend meeting once a week throughout the summer.

A mentor is a valuable resource, offering guidance and support as you navigate your new work environment and career path. Previous interns have reported forming meaningful relationships with their mentors, significantly benefiting their personal and professional development.

## What are cohort projects? How much time do I spend working on that?
A cohort project is an experiential project that supplements your primary departmental responsibilities. We often collaborate with teams within ITS and campus partners to sponsor projects that you and other interns will undertake together. These projects are designed to be manageable, typically requiring 4-6 hours of your weekly schedule.

You will meet project sponsors at orientation. At that point, you can choose a project that interests you, and we will match interns to projects. Participation in a cohort project extends beyond your core job responsibilities, giving you the opportunity to enhance your skill sets, explore new areas, and collaborate with your peers.

## What is the work schedule like? Are there set days that we have to be in-person?
You are expected to work 40 hours per week from Monday to Friday unless you are scheduled to be out of the office or have been approved by your supervisor and the internship lead team to take a class during the summer. This schedule includes mandatory on-site professional development sessions every Tuesday and Thursday from 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM. Don’t forget you should set aside 4-6 hours per week for cohort projects.

Some teams may work entirely remotely, while others operate on-site. Once you start reporting to your supervisor, you should work together to discuss a schedule and work format that best suits you and the needs of your team. The standard working hours for most ITS staff are from 8 a.m. to 5 p.m. EST, with an hour-long lunch break. Remember, your weekly hours should not exceed 40. During orientation, we will provide more information about time reporting.

## How often will I be working with other interns?
Some departments will host multiple interns this summer working on the same project. If you are part of one of these teams or projects, you will collaborate with other interns frequently! Conversely, other departments will host a single intern, who will be integrated into their team and primarily work alongside staff members to complete departmental tasks.

Of course, you will collaborate with fellow interns on your cohort project throughout the summer.

## Physical Office Locations
ITS has multiple office locations across campus. While your supervisor might have a specific working location for you in one of these buildings, interns are welcome to reserve working space at any of the ITS offices. You will learn more about this during orientation.

## How can I receive educational credit?
As a paid internship, we are not set up to grant college credit for participation in the program. However, if you are interested in using this internship to fulfill an academic requirement to complete an internship, let us know! We are happy to make sure the program fulfills your requirements.

## What is the policy for time off?
Interns are expected to limit time away from the program to no more than seven (7) business days for the duration of the program. The university will observe two federal holidays during the internship program: Memorial Day and Independence Day. ITS offices will be closed on those days, and you will not work.

For any other time off during the summer, you must receive approval from both your supervisor and the Internship Lead Team. While your supervisor may allow you to make up missed departmental time, please keep the following in mind:
Missed professional development time cannot be made up. You will not be paid for professional development hours you miss.

* Missed departmental time must be made up within the same week it was missed.
* Even though the university operates on a bi-weekly pay period, you cannot flex or make up time across Week 1 and Week 2 of that period. Make-up time must occur in the same calendar week as the absence.

## Can the internship duration be extended?
The program will conclude on August 6. However, it is common for interns to continue working part-time in their department after the program ends. We recommend starting discussions with your supervisor in late July about transitioning to a student temporary employee.

## Professional development
Is attendance mandatory for professional development activities? Yes, attendance and active participation in our weekly retreats are required. We expect all interns to demonstrate professionalism by being punctual and present for these sessions. Excessive absences or habitual tardiness are viewed as unprofessional and may lead to disciplinary measures.

## Does the program provide food on in-person days?
Professional development retreats will be from 9:00-11:00 AM on Tuesdays and Thursdays weekly. Lunch is not provided. On occasion, we may provide coffee and light snacks.

## Can professional development sessions be recorded?
Professional development sessions are always held in person and will not be recorded.

## Is parking covered for interns?
Interns are responsible for their own parking and transportation to professional development retreats. We will be at the Central Campus Classroom Building for most professional development retreats, and there is plenty of nearby parking available. You may also opt to take the bus on campus. Parking at Arbor Lakes is free, if that is your assigned or preferred office space. If you will be reporting to an office location, discuss with your supervisor what parking options are available to you.

## Orientation
Please provide more details about orientation.
On May 7, you will attend an in-person session at Arbor Lakes. Please see Claire Tucker’s emails or calendar invites for more information about the location.

The day starts with a networking opportunity to meet other interns over coffee and donuts. The remainder of the day will be filled with activities to help you prepare for your first few days, as well as get to know your fellow cohort. Please dress nicely; our Communications team will take professional headshots and a group picture of the cohort. You will also have lunch with your supervisor or a team member. 

Your first day working in your department is Friday, May 8. Be sure to discuss the arrangements for Friday with your supervisor on Thursday to ensure you are well-prepared for the morning.

## Will there be an orientation process to familiarize us with the work?
In addition to the internship program orientation, each department will have onboarding activities of its own. These will likely involve plenty of time with your coach. Your coach is a person assigned to help you learn your job and answer your questions as you get up to speed on your new role. Onboarding activities will vary by department and position, based on your needs and the demands of your position. 


## How will my performance be evaluated throughout the internship?
At the start of the program, your supervisor should provide you with a project plan outlining your responsibilities. You will have regular meetings with your supervisor or coach, which will typically include performance feedback. Later in the summer, you will undergo a Performance Evaluation. During this evaluation, you will review your performance, receive feedback from your supervisor, and discuss how well you have met the responsibilities outlined in your project plan.

If you have any questions about your performance evaluation or what your supervisor expects of you, be sure to talk with them directly within your first few days.

## What level of responsibility can I expect in this internship?
The level of responsibility will differ depending on the position, but all interns will be integral members of a professional team and will perform work comparable to that of their colleagues. You are expected to complete your assigned tasks and conduct yourself in accordance with the ITS Values.
