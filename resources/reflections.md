# Reflections 

Each week you will be asked to reflect on your progress and experience. What we are looking for is for you to tell us (your supervisor and coach **see below) the story of your week. What you reflect on will be different from week to week, but we will always ask you to reflect on the following:

### Weekly Whats
- What went well?
- What did you learn?
- What could have gone better? (from a group perspective)
- What can I improve now, or in the near future? (from a personal perspective)

We are interested in how you feel about your progress learning, your work for your department, your ITS cohort project work, and most importantly, how you are doing. We are also interested in your thoughts on the program, and how we can improve it.

Expect to take at least 20 minutes to write your reflection. While they can be as long as you like, we expect that your reflections are full enough to give us a good idea of how you are doing. We will be reading your reflections, and we will be giving you feedback on them. We will also be using your reflections to help us plan (or adjust) your next week of learning. 

&&& Reflections can be submitted via commit to your [reflections.md](reflections.md) file in your weekly reflections repo.

- Apprenticeship Patterns
I resonate with Dave's story in the chapter, where he expressed that swallowing his pride and exposing his ignorance to the experienced programmers are the toughest part during the apprenticeship. When I am learning a new coding language, often time I'd feel embarrassed to show my code to more experienced people. It is, however, more comfortable for me to show it to my peers and collaborate with them. Maybe it's because that we are all learning, I would not feel like my mistake would be judged. 

Also, I enjoy the feeling of being exposed to new information (I like the "drinking from that firehose of new information" metaphor). I think I learn the fastest when I gather and apply information for different channel. For example, I can learn from a video and apply a rails function to a hobby project. I can also watch a video on youtube about people building something cool with a little advanced rails function, and read article on how this method is better than the other. The more sources I am exposed to, the better I learn.
Overall, I think that being humble and inquisitive are important during software apprenticeship.
